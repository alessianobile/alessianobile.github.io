No 1 About the Project

An important part of the online banking platform of Illimity bank is the user’s personal homepage. In this project, the goal was to design the board containing the most important information the user wants to see right after logging in.

The information is presented to the user in the form of different cards on his/her personal board, both in the web and in the mobile versions. The cards contain all the information about the customer's money and saving goals, as well as the outcomes of his/her recent movements and his/her monthly report.

No 2 Project Description

The cards offer a fast look on some new information that the user can further explore. The text on the cards was accurately chosen to be clear and essential, avoiding frustration on the first approach with the system. Three categories of cards, associated with different aspects of the online banking experience, were designed.

The first type of cards shows information about “Saving Projects”. Indeed, the system allows the user to create these projects, assigning them a required budget and a deadline. The user can then direct his savings (e.g., by rounding expenses) to them over time. The cards were designed to clearly show all the information about the project, from its name and its type, to, importantly, which amount is missing to reach the goal.

Another important category of cards deals with "Spending limits". The user can set monthly spending limits on his bank account, both overall and by category. Cards effectively show the current state of the limits, giving the user control of the expenses of his/her choice: they show the chosen amount of money, the relative shopping category and a progress line that represents the money spent.

The "Goal achieved!" cards are bigger: they show the date of the achievement and the category the achievement belongs to (Transports, Sport and Travel are some of the categories in which it can be automatically placed by the system).

The "Spending limit" cards were instead designed to control the expenses that the user had previously set limits on: they show the chosen amount of money, the relative shopping category and a progress line that represents the money spent.
