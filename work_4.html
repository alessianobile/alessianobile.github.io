Re-design of Rome's public transport website

See the detail

No 1 About the Project

Re-design of the website with an improved organization of the content and a new user interface.

The goal was to address the main limitations of the current, old-fashioned, website. The proposal had to be both for landscape and portrait resolutions, and also offer a native app version.

No 2Project Description

Among the major critical issues, there was an excessive amount of elements and a general confusion in the arrangement of those elements (i.e., absence of hierarchies).

The original website is shown down below.

The redesign of the system was done by a thorough usability analysis and the implementation of UX solutions to eliminate the issues found with the analysis.

  • Ads were distracting too much the user from the main content: they are removed from the top of the page and moved afterwards, to enhance the visibility of the most important elements.
  • There were confusing menu voices on top. To fix it, features such as language selection and others are included in a brand new top bar, located on the right before the main menu.
  • A large amount of unorganized information was spread throughout the page. As a solution, many elements are incorporated into the main menu, that is now more visible and more easily accessible.
  • The main reasons why users visit the website, that are the ticket search feature and the news, were hidden by the other elements. They are now at the center of the page.
  • The images on the sides (left and right), with almost no useful content and no link, limited the width of the website: in the new version, they are removed to make more room for the main elements of the page.
  • The information provided by the twitter feed was redundant with respect to the news. It is now substituted as a simple icon in the footer.

The portrait version of the website was provided with an hamburger menu and a different type of interation to scroll the news (use of scroll arrows) to make the navigation in browser with smartphone easier.

For the same reason, the footer was designed with sub-voices that could be chosen through a dropdown.

The native app version featured a navbar and the same type of interation for scrolling the news (use of scroll arrows) that we could find in the portrait website.
