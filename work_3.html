Donato: The Hospital Virtual Assistant

See the detail

No 1 About the Project

Design of an innovative solution to improve the waiting experience for patients and the interaction between the patients and their doctor. The project won the competition organized by the Galeazzi Hospital, an important clinical institute in Milan, Italy.

An initial brief with the managers of the hospital clarified the main issues with the experience of the patients: the uncertainty about the expected waiting time, the poor use of technological solutions for the staff and the patients, as well as the difficulties in the interaction between patients and doctors.

No 2Project Description

The designed solution is a mobile application called "Donato - The device at your service", to be used by the patients with a reservation for a medical visit. The application runs on a phablet device that has to be provided by the hospital: this allows both to standardize the experience, but especially to include elders who have no ability of downloading an application on their smartphone.

The patient goes to a designated location inside the hospital to pick up a Donato device, by scanning his health insurance card. From that moment, Donato will show the patients information about his next visit and, thanks to the integration with the already existing in-door hospital navigation infrastructure, it can guide him/her to the waiting room, the bar, or the toilet.

Donato's homepage allows the user to monitor the waiting time and the number of people that should still be visited before him/her. This helps in reducing the stress of the waiting time, by reducing the uncertainty perceived by the user, which was determined to be the most critical issue thanks to onsite interviews with patients.

Once the patient reaches the waiting room, Donato provides him/her with different entertainment services, to alleviate the boredom of the waiting time. The entertainment services were designed and made accessible via interactions with the tab bar. A vast range of activities can reduce the stress of the waiting time: mandala coloring games, lists of short readings, crossword puzzles, personalized virtual aquaria.

A specific section was designed to make the patients comfortable with the doctor about their illness during their waiting time.

The "Tell the doctor" section was designed for every patient to write information about his health problems even before the visit, allowing the doctor to slowly digest all the information, and avoiding any feeling of shame the patient could get when directly reporting health problems during the actual visit.

The patient can always visit Donato's homepage to check on the waiting time and the number of people before him.

Donato will inform the patient when his turn has come: through proximity sensors (beacons) in the hospital, Donato is in fact capable of knowing that the previous patient has left the room and therefore completed his visit.
